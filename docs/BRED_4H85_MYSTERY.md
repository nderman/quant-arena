# The bred-4h85 Mystery — Why Can't We Replicate It?

## What It Is
bred-4h85 is an AI-generated trading engine for Polymarket 5-minute crypto binary markets (BTC/ETH/SOL "up or down" in a 5-minute window). It was generated by our breeder (Gemini analyst + Claude coder) and has been the #1 performer in our simulated arena for 60+ rounds across 6+ days.

## Performance (60 engine-rounds, 20 rounds × 3 coins)
- **+$4905 total PnL** (from $50 starting cash per round per coin)
- **76% round-level win rate** (45/59 firing rounds were net positive)
- **+$83.14 mean PnL per firing round**
- **33% per-candle win rate** but still hugely profitable due to asymmetric payoffs

## The Market
- Polymarket 5M binary markets: "Will BTC price go UP or DOWN in this 5-minute window?"
- Two tokens per market: UP and DOWN, each with independent orderbooks
- Settlement: $1 per share if correct, $0 if wrong
- Fee model: quartic formula `fee = amount × 0.25 × (P × (1-P))²` — near zero at extreme prices (5-18¢), max 1.56% at 50¢
- Markets rotate every 5 minutes (new token pair each candle)

## The Strategy (What The Code Says)
DCA (dollar-cost-average) into extreme underdog tokens (priced 5-18¢), hold to settlement. Up to 4 entries per candle at $5 each.

## The Bugs (What The Code Actually Does)

### Bug 1: Single-book price inversion
```typescript
const upPrice = tick.midPrice;        // ← uses whichever token's tick fired
const downPrice = 1 - upPrice;       // ← WRONG: UP + DOWN ≠ $1.00 on PM
```
The engine treats `tick.midPrice` as the UP price regardless of which token the tick is for. When a DOWN tick arrives with midPrice 0.90, the engine thinks UP = 0.90, DOWN = 0.10.

### Bug 2: Wrong token price for DOWN entries
```typescript
const downAsk = 1 - tick.bestBid;    // ← uses inverted bid from WRONG book
```
When buying DOWN, it uses `1 - tick.bestBid` where tick.bestBid is from whichever book the tick is for — not the DOWN book.

### Bug 3: Direction-dependent firing
Because of Bug 1:
- When an UP tick arrives with UP midPrice in [0.05, 0.18] → `extremeUp = true` → buys upTokenId at tick.bestAsk (UP's real ask) → **FILLS CORRECTLY**
- When a DOWN tick arrives with DOWN midPrice in [0.05, 0.18] → `extremeUp = true` (bug: thinks UP = 0.18) → buys upTokenId at tick.bestAsk (DOWN's ask ~0.19) → **walkBook REJECTS** (UP's actual ask is ~0.82, limit 0.19 < 0.82)
- When a DOWN tick arrives with DOWN midPrice in [0.82, 0.95] → `extremeDown = true` → buys downTokenId at `1 - tick.bestBid` → **SOMETIMES FILLS** (approximate price)

**Net effect: bred-4h85 overwhelmingly buys the UP side.** Over 12 BTC rounds: 124 UP buys vs 5 DOWN buys (96% UP bias).

### Bug 4: Random maker/taker split
```typescript
const useTaker = Math.random() < this.takerPreference; // 85% taker
```
Uses `Math.random()` (not seeded) for maker/taker decision. In our seeded-RNG sim, this is the only source of non-determinism.

## What Makes It Win (Our Best Understanding)

### 1. Asymmetric payoff at extreme prices
At 18¢ entry: win = +$0.82/share, loss = -$0.18/share (4.6:1 payoff ratio).
At 5¢ entry: win = +$0.95/share, loss = -$0.05/share (19:1 payoff ratio).
Even at 33% candle WR, EV per share at 18¢ = 0.33 × $0.82 - 0.67 × $0.18 = +$0.15.

### 2. Intra-candle pyramiding (DCA ladder)
Buys up to 4 entries per candle at progressively lower prices as the underdog compresses. Average entry is lower than first entry → better payoff ratio.

### 3. Accidental UP-side bias matching BTC's recent regime
BTC has been net-downward during our observation period. When BTC drops, the UP side becomes the cheap underdog. bred-4h85's bug makes it buy UP 96% of the time → it's accidentally aligned with the fade direction.

### 4. Accidental warm-up period
The tick.midPrice gate doesn't fire until specific book conditions exist. At round start, books are initializing → tick.midPrice doesn't land in [0.05, 0.18] → bred naturally waits ~30 minutes before first entry, skipping early-round transient prices that lose.

### 5. Lower per-candle risk
bred-4h85 averages 1.6 entries per candle (~$8 risk). Our clean copies fire 3-4 entries ($15-20 risk). bred survives early losers; clean copies go bust.

## What We Tested (All Failed)

### Hypothesis 1: "The edge is the 5-18¢ entry band"
**Engine:** dca-extreme-v1 (clean dual-book reads, same 5-18¢ band, 4 entries/candle)
**Result:** -$132 total over 46 firings (33% WR). **Failed.**

### Hypothesis 2: "The edge is the narrow 15-20¢ sub-band"
**Observation:** bred-4h85's Round 1 average entry was $0.186 (mostly 15-20¢).
**Engine:** dca-edge-entry-v1 (15-20¢ only, no pyramid block)
**Result:** -$175 total over 6 firings (0% round WR). **Falsified.** Later found the avg was biased by one round — bred uses the FULL 5-18¢ range.

### Hypothesis 3: "The edge is pyramiding (multiple entries per candle)"
**Change:** Removed `if (existing && existing.shares > 0) return [];` from dca-extreme-v1
**Result:** Improved from -$99 to +$78 over 7 rounds. **Helped but didn't close the gap.** bred was +$1970 over the same period.

### Hypothesis 4: "The edge is trend-following (buy the side momentum favors)"
**Engine:** dca-trend-extreme-v1 (same band + momentum gate: only buy when Binance moving in your direction)
**Result:** -$59 total over 12 firings. **Failed.**

### Hypothesis 5: "The edge is direction-aligned FADE (buy the losing side, bet on reversal)"
**Observation:** bred's 96% UP bias in a down-trending BTC = buying the losing side (fade).
**Engine:** dca-fade-v1 (same band + explicit fade gate: buy the side opposite to Binance momentum)
**Result:** -$139 total over 4 firings (0% WR). **Worst engine in the arena. Conclusively falsified.**

### Hypothesis 6: "The edge is a tighter regime gate"
**Change:** Tightened dca-settle-v1's regime gate (90s hysteresis + 15bps momentum floor)
**Result:** dca-settle went from +$52 to -$743. **Made things worse.**

### Hypothesis 7: "The edge is warm-up (skip early-round transients)"
**Observation:** bred-4h85 naturally waits ~30 min. dca-extreme-v1 fires at minute 1 and busts in 3 candles.
**Change:** Added `currentRegime(300) === "UNKNOWN"` gate (blocks first 5 min of buffer filling)
**Result:** Still -$98 per round on BTC. **Helped survival but didn't fix WR.**

### Hypothesis 8: "The edge is reduced per-candle risk (fewer entries)"
**Change:** Reduced dca-extreme-v1 from 4 to 2 entries per candle.
**Result:** Still busting at -$49 per coin per round. **Didn't help.**

## The Remaining Mystery

After 8 hypotheses, bred-4h85 produces +$83/round while the closest clean variant produces -$3/round on the same mechanism in the same rounds. The gap is ~$86/round = $5200 over 60 engine-rounds.

**What we HAVEN'T tested:**
1. **Tick-driven entry timing**: bred fires when `tick.midPrice` enters the range. Our clean versions fire when `book.asks[0].price` is in range. These trigger at different moments — bred catches the exact tick when a book update pushes the price into range, while the clean version fires on any tick where the book happens to show that price.

2. **The specific walkBook rejection pattern from Bug 2**: bred's DOWN entries almost always get rejected by walkBook (wrong price). This means bred effectively ONLY succeeds on UP entries via UP ticks. This is a very specific subset of all possible entries — maybe this subset happens to have higher WR.

3. **Math.random taker/maker split**: 85% taker, 15% maker. Our clean versions use deterministic splits. The random element might accidentally avoid some bad fills.

4. **The exact `modelProb = price + 0.03` vs our `price + 0.02`**: bred uses +3% edge assumption, we use +2%. This changes the feeAdjustedEdge gate slightly.

5. **"Hold both" guard**: bred has `if (holdingBoth) return [];` which prevents entries when holding both UP and DOWN. Our clean versions check per-side (`if (existing && existing.shares > 0)`). Different blocking behavior.
